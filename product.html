  Public Transport Rules

          Stand on the right, walk on the left – On escalators in the Tube, always stand on the right side.

          Tap in and out – Use an Oyster card or contactless payment when entering and exiting public transport.

          No loud conversations or music – Keep noise levels low, especially on the Tube.

          Give up priority seats – Offer your seat to elderly, disabled, or pregnant passengers.


  General Etiquette

          Queue patiently – Londoners take queuing (lining up) seriously. Cutting in line is considered rude.

          Mind the gap – Be careful of the space between the train and the platform when boarding.

          Keep to the left on pavements (sidewalks) – In crowded areas, walk on the left to avoid blocking others.


  Laws & Regulations

          No jaywalking – Cross roads only at pedestrian crossings.

          No public drinking in some areas – Alcohol consumption is banned on public transport and in certain zones.

          Smoking bans – Smoking is not allowed in indoor public spaces, including pubs, restaurants, and the Tube.


  Social Customs

          Say "Sorry" and "Thank you" – Politeness is highly valued in London.

          Tipping is optional but appreciated – In restaurants, a 10-15% tip is common if service is not included.

          Respect personal space – Avoid unnecessary physical contact, especially with strangers.
